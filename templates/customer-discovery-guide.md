## Interview Script Template

### Opening (2 min)

**Introduction:**
> "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time. I'm researching how people [activity related to problem]. I don't have a product to sell—I'm trying to understand if this is a real problem worth solving. Anything you share will help me decide whether to build something. Is that okay?"

**Set Context:**
> "This will take about 15-20 minutes. Feel free to be completely honest—negative feedback is actually more helpful than positive."

---

### Problem Discovery (10 min)

**Question 1: Tell me about the last time you [experienced problem situation].**
- What was the situation?
- What did you do?
- How did that work out?
- How did you feel?

**Question 2: How often does this happen?**
- Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely
- When was the last time?
- What triggers it?

**Question 3: What do you currently do to solve this?**
- Walk me through your current process
- What tools/products/services do you use?
- What are the pain points in your current solution?
- What would an ideal solution look like?

**Question 4: If you could wave a magic wand...**
- What would change?
- What would stay the same?
- What's preventing you from this ideal state?

---

### JTBD Probe (5 min)

**Functional Job:**
- What are you ultimately trying to accomplish?
- What would "done" look like?
- How do you measure success?

**Emotional Job:**
- How do you want to feel when this is solved?
- What frustrates you most about the current situation?
- What would make you feel [relieved/satisfied/etc.]?

**Social Job:**
- How do you want others to see you in this context?
- What would success signal to others?
- Who else is affected by this problem?

---

### Forces of Progress (5 min)

**PUSH Forces (Current dissatisfaction):**
- What's frustrating about your current approach?
- What happens if you do nothing?
- What are the costs of the status quo?

**PULL Forces (Attraction of new solution):**
- What would make you switch to a new solution?
- What features would be must-haves?
- What would success with a new solution look like?

**ANXIETY Forces (Fear of switching):**
- What concerns would you have about trying something new?
- What could go wrong?
- What would make you hesitate?

**HABIT Forces (Inertia):**
- Why haven't you changed already?
- What's keeping you in your current approach?
- What would have to change for you to switch?

---

### Closing (3 min)

**Question 5: If I built a solution that [value proposition], would you use it?**
- Likely / Maybe / Unlikely
- What would prevent you from using it?
- What would convince you?

**Question 6: Would you be willing to pay for this?**
- How much would it need to cost?
- What pricing model makes sense?
- Would you commit to a pilot/beta?

**Letter of Intent Ask:**
> "I'm considering building this. Would you be willing to sign a non-binding letter of intent saying you'd try it when ready? No commitment, just helps me validate there's real demand."

**Referral Request:**
> "Do you know 2-3 other people who have this problem I could talk to?"

---

## Observation Checklist

### During Interview
- [ ] Interview conducted in customer's environment (if possible)
- [ ] Watched them perform the activity (shadow if possible)
- [ ] Collected concrete stories, not opinions
- [ ] Asked "why" 5 times to get to root cause
- [ ] Did NOT pitch solution
- [ ] Listened 80%, talked 20%

### Red Flags Heard
- [ ] "That sounds interesting" (polite, not urgent)
- [ ] "I could see myself using that" (hypothetical)
- [ ] "If it had X feature..." (future scope creep)
- [ ] "Let me know when it's ready" (no commitment)

### Green Flags Heard
- [ ] "I've been looking for something like this"
- [ ] "I spent $X trying to solve this"
- [ ] "This happens all the time"
- [ ] "Can I use it now?"
- [ ] "I'd pay $X for this"

## Phase 2 Go/No-Go Decision

### Validation Criteria

**Required (All must pass):**
- [ ] 40+ interviews completed
- [ ] 60%+ confirm problem is urgent
- [ ] 60%+ confirm problem is frequent
- [ ] 3+ concrete stories documented

**Target Metrics:**
- [ ] 40%+ willing to pay
- [ ] 10%+ signed LoI
- [ ] Clear JTBD identified
- [ ] Target segment defined

### GO Signals
- [ ] Customers describing problem in their own words
- [ ] "Fish jumping into boat" (inbound interest)
- [ ] Customers already spending money on workarounds
- [ ] Clear persona emerging
- [ ] Pattern consistency across interviews

### NO-GO Signals
- [ ] "Interesting" responses, no urgency
- [ ] Hypothetical interest, no commitments
- [ ] Problem too rare or minor
- [ ] No willingness to pay
- [ ] "Everyone's problem" (no specific persona)
