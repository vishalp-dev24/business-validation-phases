### PHASE 2: PROBLEM VALIDATION (Customer Development + JTBD)

**Framework Applied:**
- Customer Discovery (Steve Blank) ✓
- Jobs-to-be-Done (Christensen) ✓
- Forces of Progress Analysis ✓

**VALIDATING THE PROBLEM: Anxiety Prediction**

**Interview Target:** 40+ anxiety sufferers + 10+ clinicians

**PROBLEM DISCOVERY QUESTIONS APPLIED:**

Q1: "Tell me about the last time you felt an anxiety episode coming on."
- Expected response: "I didn't know until it was happening"
- KEY INSIGHT: The premise assumes people WANT prediction - not validated

Q2: "How often do you wish you had advance warning?"
- Expected response: "All the time" from people WITH anxiety
- COUNTERPOINT: Would advance warning actually help? Could increase anticipatory anxiety

**SKILL ISSUE #4: NO "WILL THIS ACTUALLY HELP?" VALIDATION**
- Framework asks if problem exists
- Doesn't ask if SOLUTION type would be beneficial
- Anxiety prediction could INCREASE suffering (anticipatory anxiety)

**FORCES OF PROGRESS ANALYSIS:**

| Force | Assessment | Issue |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| PUSH (Dissatisfaction) | High - anxiety is painful | ✓ Validated |
| PULL (New solution) | High - people want relief | ⚠️ But predictive intervention efficacy UNPROVEN |
| ANXIETY (Fear of switching) | HIGH - privacy fears, efficacy doubts | ✗ Not addressed in framework |
| HABIT (Inertia) | MEDIUM - current coping mechanisms exist | ✓ Addressable |

**JOBS-TO-BE-DONE ANALYSIS:**

Functional Job: "Help me manage my anxiety episodes"
- Current solutions: Medication, therapy, breathing apps, grounding techniques
- Proposed solution: 30-minute advance warning + intervention
- GAP: Is advance warning + intervention MORE effective than alternatives?

Emotional Job: "Help me feel in control of my anxiety"
- Prediction could provide control OR increase fear ("it's coming")
- DEPENDS on individual psychology

Social Job: "Help me function normally despite anxiety"
- 30-min warning could help prepare socially
- Or could trigger avoidance behaviors

**SKILL ISSUE #5: NO EVIDENCE-BASED VALIDATION REQUIREMENT**
- Framework doesn't require "prove your intervention works"
- Mental health interventions need evidence
- Self-reported willingness ≠ clinical efficacy

**CUSTOMER SOURCING FOR THIS SPECIFIC IDEA:**

| Source | Approach | Challenge |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Reddit (r/anxiety, r/mentalhealth) | Post research questions | Ethical: researching vulnerable population |
| Therapy practices | Partner for patient recruitment | HIPAA compliance, IRB approval if research |
| Anxiety support groups | Attend meetings | Privacy concerns, group dynamics |
| Direct outreach | Social media ads | FDA regulations on promoting investigational devices |

**SKILL ISSUE #6: NO MENTAL HEALTH RECRUITING GUIDANCE**
- Template suggests standard "$50 Amazon gift card" approach
- Doesn't address ethics of incentivizing vulnerable populations
- No mention of IRB/ethics review
- Missing: "Are you putting participants at risk?"

**PHASE 2 GO/NO-GO FOR ANXIETY PREDICTOR:**

## CRITICAL FAILURES IDENTIFIED

### 1. HEALTHCARE BLIND SPOT (CRITICAL)

**What Failed:**
The skill has NO recognition that health tech requires completely different validation.

**Evidence:**
- No FDA/regulatory pathway checklist
- No clinical evidence requirements
- No HIPAA compliance guidance
- No medical advisor requirement
- Treats "anxiety predictor" like photo-sharing app

**Severity:** CRITICAL - could lead to illegal operations

---

### 2. EFFICACY VS INTEREST CONFUSION (CRITICAL)

**What Failed:**
Framework measures CUSTOMER INTEREST, not PRODUCT EFFICACY.

**Evidence:**
- Fake door tests measure signups, not health outcomes
- Sean Ellis score measures sentiment, not clinical outcomes
- NPS correlates poorly with medical efficacy
- Willingness to pay ≠ Willingness to adhere to treatment

**Impact for Anxiety Predictor:**
- 90% of surveyed users might say "I'd pay $50/month" 
- But only 30% might actually see clinical benefit
- Framework would approve GO based on interest
- Real business fails (or worse: causes harm)

**Severity:** CRITICAL - false positive validation

---

### 3. MVP FRAMEWORK INAPPLICABILITY (HIGH)

**What Failed:**
Wizard of Oz / Concierge MVP assumes you can MANUALLY deliver the core value.

**For Anxiety Predictor:**
- Humans cannot reliably predict anxiety in others
- Core value IS the ML prediction
- No MVP type can validate the actual product
- Minimum testable product = clinical pilot study

**Missing:** Framework should flag "If core value requires ML inference, standard MVPs don't apply"

**Severity:** HIGH - leads to wasted effort on impossible MVPs

---

### 4. NO ADVERSE OUTCOME CONSIDERATION (HIGH)

**What Failed:**
No validation of "What if this makes things WORSE?"

**For Anxiety Predictor:**
- Prediction accuracy 60% = 40% false alarms
- False alarms could cause anticipatory anxiety
- Intervention advice could be harmful if wrong
- Framework asks "Will people like it?" not "Is it safe?"

**Missing Questions:**
- What's your false positive rate tolerance?
- Have you conducted risk analysis?
- Who reviews clinical safety?
- What's your adverse event reporting process?

**Severity:** HIGH - patient safety risk

---

### 5. RETENTION BENCHMARKS WRONG (MEDIUM)

**What Failed:**
Mental health apps use DIFFERENT success metrics.

**Evidence:**
- Framework: "Day 7 retention >30% is good"
- Mental health reality: "Day 7 retention <10% might mean people recovered"
- Paradox: Best outcome = users graduate (churn)

**Missing:** Segment-specific benchmarks for health apps

**Severity:** MEDIUM - misleading metrics

---

### 6. NO CONSUMER VS REGULATED DEVICE DISTINCTION (MEDIUM)

**What Failed:**
No way to specify "This is a general wellness app" vs "This is a regulated medical device"

**Anxiety Predictor could be:**
- General wellness (no FDA): Just a relaxation aid
- Class I: Low risk, some oversight
- Class II: Moderate risk, 510(k) required
- Class III: High risk, PMA required

**Framework treats all the same.**

**Severity:** MEDIUM - legal compliance risk

---

### 7. NO TWO-SIDED HEALTHCARE MODEL (MEDIUM)

**What Failed:**
Healthcare has TWO customers always: Patient AND Provider

**Framework:**
- Assumes single customer type
- No "patient/user + clinician" validation
- No reimbursement pathway validation
- Missing: "Will doctors recommend this?"

**Severity:** MEDIUM - incomplete validation
